Chaul Chnam Thmey: A Joyful Khmer Tradition

A Celebration of Renewal: Cambodian New Year, or Chaul Chnam Thmey, isn't just a time of festivities—it's a heartfelt tradition that brings communities together. For Cambodian families, this three-day event is filled with vibrant culture, spiritual reflection, and joyful connection.

Marking the transition from the old year to the new, this holiday symbolizes renewal and gratitude. Across Cambodia and in Khmer communities worldwide, people come together to honor ancestors, make offerings at temples, cleanse their spirits with water blessings, and celebrate with dance, games, and traditional cuisine. It's a time to reconnect—with our roots, with each other, and with the hopes of the coming year.

Three Days of Cultural Richness:

Day 1 - Moha Sangkran: Welcoming the New Year by cleaning homes and visiting temples to offer prayers and light incense, invoking blessings from the new angel of the year.

Day 2 - Veareak Vanabat: A day dedicated to kindness and charity—offering gifts to the less fortunate and spending time with family in gratitude and service.

Day 3 - Vearak Laeung Sak: The day of renewal and blessing. Water plays a key role here—cleansing statues, elders' hands, and symbolizing a fresh start for the year ahead.

Why it matters: In a globalized world, cultural preservation is more vital than ever. Khmer New Year serves as a bridge between generations and keeps our identity alive.
